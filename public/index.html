The Challenge

285 million people worldwide navigate a world not designed for them. The core challenge is not just impaired vision, but the loss of independent agency.

285,000,000

A world built on visual assumptions becomes an invisible maze. Street signs, digital interfaces, social cues—all designed for eyes that see. Every journey demands constant vigilance, every interaction requires assistance, and independence becomes a privilege rather than a right.

Navigation Difficulties

Unfamiliar environments pose constant risks. Collisions with obstacles, uneven surfaces, and traffic create a perpetual mental load.

  • Unpredictable terrain changes without warning
  • No real-time awareness of approaching vehicles
  • Indoor navigation requires memorization or assistance

Information Access

Essential information—signs, menus, documents—remains locked behind a visual wall, requiring constant third-party assistance to decode.

  • Street signs and emergency notices invisible
  • Menu boards and product labels inaccessible
  • Digital interfaces often poorly optimized

Limited Independence

Reliance on others for simple daily tasks erodes autonomy. The inability to move freely without a guide restricts personal freedom.

  • Simple errands become complex operations
  • Travel planning requires extensive preparation
  • Spontaneous decisions often impossible

Social Isolation

The difficulty in recognizing faces and reading social cues creates an invisible barrier to participation in community life.

  • Face recognition requires verbal confirmation
  • Body language and expressions remain hidden
  • Group conversations difficult to follow

Product Capabilities

Six core intelligence systems working in unison to deliver real-world assistive performance.

Intelligent Navigation

Real-time path guidance using sensor fusion and object detection. Identifies safe corridors, detects obstacles at multiple body levels, and provides proactive spatial audio cues.

Real-Time Safety Awareness

Continuous environmental scanning with YOLO detection. Identifies people, vehicles, stairs, and hazards in real time — no cloud round-trip required.

Adaptive Alerts

Smart priority logic filters noise from signal. Alerts are context-aware — verbosity, urgency, and frequency adapt dynamically to environment and situation.

Offline-First Privacy

All core processing runs on-device. No data leaves the hardware without consent. Full functionality in zero-network environments — privacy by architecture, not policy.

Emergency Response System

Detects distress signals and triggers automatic emergency SMS with GPS coordinates via the companion app. Caregiver alerts without user action required.

Context-Aware Announcements

Objects are announced intelligently — not everything, only what matters. The system understands scene context to eliminate audio fatigue and deliver useful information.

Architecture Flow

A deterministic, offline-first processing loop. Every perception cycle completes on-device — input to speech in milliseconds.

01Camera Input30fps

Continuous video frames captured from the RPi camera module at 30fps.

02Edge ProcessingOpenCV

Frame preprocessing, resizing, and normalisation done on-device before inference.

03Object DetectionYOLO

YOLO model identifies objects, people, and hazards in real time — no cloud needed.

04Context FilteringPriority Logic

Smart filtering decides what matters — reducing noise and preventing alert fatigue.

05Risk ScoringSensor Fusion

Camera + ultrasonic/IR data fused to assign urgency scores to detected threats.

06Speech OutputeSpeak NG

High-priority alerts converted to audio via a non-blocking threaded TTS pipeline.

↺ loop
Continuous Loop

Cycle repeats instantly — fully offline, no external dependency, running indefinitely on-device.

Healthcare Impact

Six dimensions of measurable real-world impact on the lives of visually impaired individuals.

Safer Mobility

Real-time hazard detection actively prevents accidents before they occur. Objects, obstacles, and dangers are identified and announced with enough lead time to react.

Independent Navigation

Enables fully independent movement without reliance on human guides. Users navigate public spaces, commute, and travel safely on their own terms.

Reduced Anxiety

Reduces travel-related stress, fear, and anxiety. Knowing hazards are monitored continuously gives users confidence to move through the world.

Increased Dignity

Reduces caregiver dependency and promotes equality. Independence restores dignity — users live on their own terms without constant reliance on others.

Real-World Usability

Empowers users to commute, shop, and engage in social activities independently. The system acts as a reliable invisible guide, expanding the user's world.

Affordable Accessibility

Safety and technology-driven inclusive lifestyle. Vision AID directly raises the floor for what's possible — not just assistive, but transformative.
