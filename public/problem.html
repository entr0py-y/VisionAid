The Challenge

285 million people worldwide navigate a world not designed for them. The core challenge is not just impaired vision, but the loss of independent agency.

Navigation Difficulties

Unfamiliar environments pose constant risks. Collisions with obstacles, uneven surfaces, and traffic create a perpetual mental load for the user.

Information Access

Essential information—signs, menus, documents—remains locked behind a visual wall, requiring constant third-party assistance to decode.

Limited Independence

Reliance on others for simple daily tasks erodes autonomy. The inability to move freely without a guide restricts personal freedom.

Social Isolation

The difficulty in recognizing faces and reading social cues creates an invisible barrier to participation in community life.
